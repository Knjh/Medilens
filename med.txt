Possible Medicines for Bladder Cancer:

Based on the information provided, the presence of pus cells and hematuria in urine may indicate bladder cancer. However, it is important to note that this is just a potential diagnosis, and further medical evaluation is necessary for confirmation. Here are some possible medicines that may be recommended for bladder cancer:

 Chemotherapy:
     Cisplatin
     Carboplatin
     Paclitaxel
     Gemcitabine
 Immunotherapy:
     BCG (Bacillus Calmette-Guerin)
     Atezolizumab
     Pembrolizumab
 Targeted Therapy:
     Erlotinib
     Gefitinib
     Afatinib
 Surgery:
     Transurethral resection of bladder tumor (TURBT)
     Cystectomy (removal of the bladder)
 Radiation Therapy:
     External beam radiation therapy
     Brachytherapy (internal radiation)

It is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional for an accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment plan. Self-medication is not recommended for bladder cancer or any other medical condition.
